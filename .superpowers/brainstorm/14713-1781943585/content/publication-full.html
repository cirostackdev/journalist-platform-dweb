Leaked Documents Reveal Hidden Contracts Between State Officials and Private Contractors

The Newsroom
June 18, 2026
Verified

For eighteen months, our journalists reviewed thousands of pages of internal documents obtained through anonymous, encrypted submissions. What emerged is a pattern of deliberate concealment spanning three administrations.

The contracts, dated between 2019 and 2024, were executed through a series of shell companies registered in Cyprus and the British Virgin Islands. None appear in any public procurement record. Multiple officials whose signatures appear on the documents declined to comment.

What the Documents Show

Among the files submitted were 847 digitally signed contract amendments, 23 internal memoranda marked "not for distribution," and a series of wire transfer confirmations totalling $387 million.

"We were told these were standard infrastructure contracts. No one asked questions. That was the point."

Independent forensic accountants retained by the newsroom confirmed the authenticity of the financial records. Three former officials, speaking on condition of anonymity, corroborated the timeline described in the documents.

The Response

When presented with the findings, the Ministry of Finance issued a one-line statement: "We do not comment on internal procurement matters." Two of the private contractors named in the documents have since been dissolved.
